charge equilibration routine.

=================================================================================
EXPECTED BEHAVIOR:
=================================================================================

At 1000 Å separation (>> van der Waals interaction range):
   - Water charges should equal monomer charges (within numerical precision)
   - He charge should be ~0 (no charge transfer possible at this distance)
   - C6 coefficients should equal monomer values (within numerical precision)

=================================================================================
ROOT CAUSE:
=================================================================================

The charge equilibration routine (get_charges from multicharge module) performs
electronegativity equilibration (EEQ) across the ENTIRE system without:

1. Distance-based cutoffs for charge transfer
2. Fragment detection to identify non-interacting subsystems
3. Convergence to monomer limits at large separations

The charges are computed globally, so even at 1000 Å:
   - He "steals" 0.0147 electrons from the water molecule
   - This changes water's charge distribution
   - Changed charges affect C6 via the zeta weighting function
   - C6 values propagate the error into dispersion energies

=================================================================================
PHYSICAL INTERPRETATION:
=================================================================================

The bug means that DFTD4 incorrectly predicts:
   - Long-range charge transfer at non-physical distances
   - Different dispersion properties for isolated molecules depending on what
     other molecules exist in the system (even at 1000 Å away!)
   - Non-converging behavior as fragments separate

This violates size-consistency and fragment separability principles.

=================================================================================
IMPACT:
=================================================================================

1. Dispersion energies for weakly-bound complexes will be incorrect
2. Potential energy surfaces won't have correct asymptotic behavior
3. Properties of molecules in a system depend on distant non-interacting species
4. Violates fundamental physical principles (size-extensivity)
